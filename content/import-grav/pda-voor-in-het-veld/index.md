[Slashdot](http://web.archive.org/web/20050207105915/http://slashdot.org/article.pl?sid=04/09/28/1340219&tid=100&tid=1): “A company in UK called Node has developed the world’s first consumer [PDA designed for use specifically in outdoor environments](http://web.archive.org/web/20050207105915/http://www.nodeexplore.com/) [[plaatje](http://web.archive.org/web/20050207105915/http://www.weblogsinc.com/common/images/3856521329796276.JPG?0.2795654670668941)]. The device is fully waterproof to 3 meters, has a 8 hour battery life, built in DGPS receiver and 1 Gig of storage. Bluetooth and WiFi come as standard as does a touch screen and either a PocketPC or Linux operating system.”

Via [Engadget](http://web.archive.org/web/20050207105915/http://www.engadget.com/entry/1534298196564954/)
